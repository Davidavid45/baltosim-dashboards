Study scope and interpretation

This dashboard is a scenario-screening tool for stakeholder review. It compares the current May 2026 MTA schedule with a CityLink frequency overlay using the same modeled trip set.

Review draft - May 13, 2026

Question answered

Where could more frequent CityLink service reduce travel or wait time?

Comparison

Current May 2026 MTA GTFS vs. BMORE BUS CityLink frequency overlay

Trip basis

40,701 BMC/MPO 2019 walk-to-transit model trips

Demand assumption

Demand, route coverage, and trip origins/destinations are held fixed

Appropriate use

Use these results to identify promising corridors, destinations, time periods, and equity screens for follow-up review.

Important limit

This is not a ridership forecast, final equity finding, or public claim about service gaps. Unrouted trips require case-by-case diagnosis.

Main takeaway

CityLink frequency changes do not transform the network average. They reduce waiting for the subset of trips that use or sit near CityLink service. Most trips do not change; 1,276 trips improve by at least 5 minutes.

What this scenario does not answer

  • Does not estimate new ridership
  • Does not model mode-choice response
  • Does not change route coverage
  • Does not change service span beyond stated headways
  • Does not diagnose all unrouted trips yet
  • Does not include bus congestion feedback

Defining "CityLink-exposed"

These are two different definitions. Reporting them separately avoids overlap and double-counting confusion.

A - Near CityLink stop

Trip's origin or destination is within one-quarter mile of at least one CityLink stop.

B - Routed on CityLink

Trip's baseline OR scenario routed path actually uses at least one CityLink route segment.

The headline metrics below are reported for the union of A and B, which captures trips near CityLink stops or actually using CityLink routes.

Read the caveats below before quoting any number to an external audience. The Study scope and interpretation panel on Tab 1 documents the data behind the current run.

Caveats

Static demand

The same trip set is routed through both networks. The model does not change rider behavior, mode choice, destination choice, or trip-making rates in response to better service.

Router-only comparison

Outputs reflect what a transit router can find on the published schedule. No congestion feedback, no bus-bunching, no real-world reliability adjustment.

2019 demand - 2026 GTFS

Demand snapshot pre-dates the GTFS network used for routing. Trip patterns may have shifted since 2019; treat the OD set as a representative sample, not a current census.

Unrouted != confirmed service gap

"Unrouted" means the router found no valid scheduled path under current settings. It does not automatically mean the trip is impossible - walk distance limits, transfer rules, and time-window settings can all drive non-routes.

Use unrouted trips as review flags

When a trip is unrouted, the next step is to inspect the origin, destination, time window, walk access, transfer rules, and nearby service. The dashboard should not assign a single cause without that follow-up review.

Definitions

Trip lifecycle

RoutedA trip for which the router found a valid scheduled path.
UnroutedA trip for which no valid scheduled path was found under current settings.
Both-routedTrip routed in baseline and scenario - the comparable subset for delta-time stats.
Newly routableRouted in scenario only.
Lost routingRouted in baseline only.

Outcomes

Improved / WorsenedTravel-time change of at least 5 min in the improvement / worsening direction.
Failure rateShare of submitted trips that are unrouted.
Average transfersMean number of transit transfers per routed trip.

Scope

CityLink-exposedUnion of "near CityLink stop" (filter A) and "routed on CityLink" (filter B). See Tab 2.
Walk-to-transitOnly access mode currently modeled. Drive-and-ride and bike-and-ride are out of scope under the present access assumption.

Methodology

Trip-weightedEach trip counts once, regardless of rider. Default in this dashboard.
Person-weightedAggregated to unique riders. Requires demographic linkage. Not yet wired. Do not cite trip-weighted breakdowns as person-level findings.

Mode-sequence diagnostics

Walk-to-transit sequences only. Drive-and-ride, bike-and-ride, and transit-to-walk loops are not modeled. Trips with no plausible walk access may fail in both networks for reasons unrelated to frequency.
