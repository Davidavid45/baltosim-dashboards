Main takeaway

CityLink frequency changes do not transform the network average. They reduce waiting for the subset of trips that use or sit near CityLink service. Most trips do not change; 1,276 trips improve by at least 5 minutes.

What this scenario does not answer

  • Does not model mode-choice response
  • Does not change route coverage
  • Does not change service span beyond stated headways
  • Does not diagnose all unrouted trips yet
  • Does not include bus congestion feedback

These are two different definitions. Reporting them separately avoids overlap and double-counting confusion.

A - Near CityLink stop

Trip's origin or destination is within one-quarter mile of at least one CityLink stop.

B - Routed on CityLink

Trip's baseline OR scenario routed path actually uses at least one CityLink route segment.

Transit-desert screen

Transit desert tracts are census tracts where transit vulnerability exceeds available transit access, identified using a hybrid method that combines relative/spatial underservice with absolute minimum-service failures. Use this as a screening layer, then confirm any claim with maps.

How we define a transit desert

The method compares demand and supply by tract: transit gap = transit vulnerability index (TVI) minus composite public transit accessibility (CPTA). A tract is flagged when it is in a high-gap spatial cluster or high-demand/low-supply quadrant, or when it fails at least three minimum-service thresholds and has above-median latent vulnerability.

Caveats

Static demand

The same trip set is routed through both networks. The model does not change rider behavior, mode choice, destination choice, or trip-making rates in response to better service.

Router-only comparison

Outputs reflect what a transit router can find on the published schedule. No congestion feedback, no bus-bunching, no real-world reliability adjustment.

2019 demand - 2026 GTFS

Demand snapshot pre-dates the GTFS network used for routing. Trip patterns may have shifted since 2019; treat the OD set as a representative sample, not a current census.

Unrouted != confirmed service gap

"Unrouted" means the router found no valid scheduled path under current settings. It does not automatically mean the trip is impossible - walk distance limits, transfer rules, and time-window settings can all drive non-routes.

Use unrouted trips as review flags

When a trip is unrouted, the next step is to inspect the origin, destination, time window, walk access, transfer rules, and nearby service. The dashboard should not assign a single cause without that follow-up review.
